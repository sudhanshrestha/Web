Impact of Games
MAY 7 , 2019 BY SUDHAN SHRESTHA



I remember playing my first game in a black and white TV with a console plugged in it and playing a Contra. It was my first time playing a game as in a console and although the game’s graphics were in tiny pixelated form, I was quickly hooked on to it. Video Games have developed quite much from then games now day come in high quality with real life graphic which make you go “Wow!”.
These games have been a digital source of entertainment these days and ages from kids to adults play them these days. These games have been making a huge impact on the society as due to these games many people children specially are spending more time in homes instead of going out and play , this also result in making the newer generation of children more socially awkward and as this industry is growing with game that are pretty much close to reality with Virtual Reality(VR) who know what will happen.

Games are very fun to play and are exciting, so we waste much of our time on them and are so into it that we spend most of our time playing them and in a blink of an eye time goes by and we don’t even notice it. Although these games have some of the negative effect these also have their own positive impact which most of us fail to notice. As most of the younger generation are into them these younger ones can get to know about various things such as the issue related to violence, call of duty and responsibility. They get rewards if they follow the rules of the games and if they try to cut line or do some illegal things inside the game which can also be related in real life, they get punished which makes them aware the negative and positives things. Playing games have also proven to be make our reflexes fast as modern First-Person Shooter games (FPS) are fast paced and need quick reflexes. Some games also provide extra knowledge about various things and even history sometimes which are good for the younger generations.

Video games create a separate alternate universe for us to enjoy if we can make them more creative and knowledgeable then it will be much better. Rather than restrict the children’s form games we should let them enjoy games which may benefit them and the adults can also enjoy these games with them in doing so the young ones minds can be sharpened ad the adults can take this time to relax and have fun.
